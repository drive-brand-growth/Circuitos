================================================================================
SYSTEM 1: MARKETING ONBOARDING ASSESSMENT
================================================================================

WHAT IT DOES:
-------------
This is a comprehensive assessment system that evaluates how well DRN Data
marketing employees understand:
- LPR (License Plate Recognition) technology
- DRN Capture platform (marketing attribution product)
- Carwash industry (main target market)
- Marketing attribution & ROI concepts
- Customer segmentation strategies
- Campaign management best practices
- Key marketing metrics (CAC, LTV, ROAS, churn rate)

WHO IT'S FOR:
-------------
- New marketing hires (Marketer, Manager, Director, VP, CMO levels)
- Employees transitioning to marketing roles
- Managers evaluating team competency

HOW IT WORKS:
-------------
1. Manager selects employee's role and milestone
   (e.g., "Marketer at 30-day check-in")

2. Agent presents 10-15 role-appropriate questions covering:
   - Knowledge (Can you recall facts?)
   - Comprehension (Can you explain concepts?)
   - Application (Can you execute tasks?)
   - Analysis (Can you diagnose problems?)
   - Evaluation (Can you critique strategies?)
   - Creation (Can you design new solutions?)

3. Manager provides employee's answers

4. Agent generates comprehensive report:
   - Overall score (0-100%)
   - Bloom's Taxonomy breakdown
   - Domain gap analysis (which areas are weak?)
   - Personalized learning plan (what to study next)
   - Manager summary (is employee on track?)

EXAMPLE QUESTIONS:
------------------
Week 1-2 (Knowledge Level):
- "What does LPR stand for?"
- "Name DRN's three core products"
- "Define CAC (Customer Acquisition Cost)"

30 Days (Analysis Level):
- "Campaign A: $5K spend, 50 leads, 5 closed deals ($100K revenue)
   Campaign B: $3K spend, 20 leads, 4 closed deals ($90K revenue)
   Which has better ROI? Show your math."

90 Days (Creation Level):
- "Design a referral campaign for a carwash with 500 active members"

SCORING SYSTEM:
---------------
- 90-100% (Exemplary): Exceeds expectations, ready for advancement
- 75-89% (Proficient): Meets expectations, on track
- 60-74% (Developing): Progressing, needs support
- 0-59% (Below Expectations): Requires immediate intervention

SYSTEM 2: SALES TRAINING SIMULATOR
================================================================================

WHAT IT DOES:
-------------
This is an interactive role-play training system where the GPT acts as
realistic, challenging buyer personas from DRN's target markets. Sales/marketing
reps practice their pitch, handle objections, and get scored on performance.

Think of it as a flight simulator for sales calls - a safe space to crash and
learn before talking to real customers.

WHO IT'S FOR:
-------------
- New sales/marketing reps learning DRN's value proposition
- Experienced reps practicing complex objections
- Managers running team training sessions

THE 6 BUYER PERSONAS:
---------------------

1. CREDIT UNION - Marketing Director (Jennifer Martinez)
   - Pain: High member acquisition cost ($600 per member), can't prove which
     marketing drives sign-ups
   - Objections: "We already have Google Analytics. Why LPR?" / "Privacy concerns"
   - Budget: $1.2M annual marketing budget

2. OIL & LUBE - VP of Marketing (Marcus Chen)
   - Pain: Low visit frequency (every 3-6 months), 40% of first-timers never return
   - Objections: "How do you track customers without emails?" / "Need ROI in 90 days"
   - Budget: $750K annual ($21K per location)

3. AUTO REPAIR - Marketing Manager (Sarah Thompson)
   - Pain: Hard to stay top-of-mind between visits, one-person marketing team
   - Objections: "Customers come when car breaks. How does tracking help?" /
     "Can we afford $10K-$15K/year?"
   - Budget: $180K annual ($36K per location)

4. CAR WASH - Director of Marketing (David Rodriguez)
   - Pain: Can't prove which campaigns work, 8% monthly churn, $80K/month ad waste
   - Objections: "We already have wash tracking. What's different?" /
     "Privacy concerns for members"
   - Budget: $1.5M annual

5. CAR DEALERSHIP - Marketing Director (Amanda Lewis)
   - Pain: Attribution nightmare, service retention weak (45%), long sales cycles
   - Objections: "We have CDK and VinSolutions. How does this add value?" /
     "How do you track test drives vs. service vs. sales?"
   - Budget: $3.2M annual ($800K per dealership)

6. FLEET MANAGEMENT - CMO (Bob Patterson)
   - Pain: 9-18 month sales cycles, complex buying committees, 25% win rate
   - Objections: "We're B2B SaaS, not retail. How does LPR apply?" /
     "We have 6sense for intent. What's different?"
   - Budget: $4.5M annual

PROGRESSIVE DIFFICULTY SYSTEM:
------------------------------

üìó WEEK 1-2 (EASY MODE) - Confidence Building

üìò WEEK 3-4 (MODERATE MODE) - Standard Training

- Goal: Prove readiness

üìï 60 DAYS (ADVANCED MODE) - Complex Deals
- Buyer is highly analytical, data-driven
- 4-5 objections + follow-up questions
- Introduces stakeholders (CFO, IT team)
- Tests closing skills
- Goal: Master complex deals

üìî 90 DAYS (EXPERT MODE) - Executive-Level
- Buyer is C-level (CMO, VP), strategic thinker
- 5+ objections, buying committee involved
- Requires consultative selling
- Long-term partnership evaluation
- Goal: Executive presence and strategic selling

üíÄ NIGHTMARE MODE - Stress Test
- Buyer is hostile, combative, distracted
- Already chose a competitor
- Meeting is a courtesy (nearly impossible to recover)
- Tests graceful recovery or exit
- Goal: Stress inoculation

WHAT GETS SCORED:
-----------------

   - Did they ask industry-specific questions?

   - Did they differentiate from competitors?

   - Did they address objections confidently?
   - Did they use proof points (case studies, data)?
   - Did they turn objections into opportunities?

   - Did they ask for a specific next step (demo, pilot, proposal)?
   - Did they create urgency?
   - Did they get commitment (date, time, who attends)?
