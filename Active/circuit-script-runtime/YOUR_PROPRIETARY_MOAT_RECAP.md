### Framework 2: Eugene Schwartz 5 Awareness Levels (Your "Marty Version")
**Origin:** Classic Schwartz Awareness model, adapted for your use cases

**The 5 Levels:**
1. **Unaware:** Don't know they have a problem (ignore them)
2. **Problem Aware:** Know traditional gyms dying, don't know solution (educate)
3. **Solution Aware:** Know boutique is answer, comparing options (prove superiority)
4. **Product Aware:** Know MetroFlex, evaluating fit (overcome objections)
5. **Most Aware:** Ready to buy, need final push (assumptive close)

**How You Use It:**
- **Judgment #17** maps prospect to awareness level
- **Messaging adapts** to their stage (Problem Aware gets GLP-1 crisis education, Most Aware gets Brian Dobson call)
- **Email templates change** based on awareness (not one-size-fits-all)

**Why It's a Moat:**
Most outreach treats everyone as "Product Aware" (HERE'S OUR SOLUTION!). You're diagnosing their awareness stage FIRST, then matching messaging. That's sophisticated.

**3 Sub-Frameworks:**

```

```

**How You Use It:**
- **Dream Outcome:** Judgment #2 (Problem Severity) + #10 (Long-Term Vision)
- **Perceived Likelihood:** Judgment #12 (Moat Recognition) + #8 (Social Proof Sensitivity)
- **Time Delay:** Judgment #4 (Timing) + #13 (Urgency Multipliers)
- **Effort:** Judgment #9 (Coach-ability) + #16 (Capital Allocation)

**Example in Email:**
