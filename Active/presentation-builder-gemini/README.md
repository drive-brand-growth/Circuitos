### Step 1: Choose Format
Gem asks you to select:
1. PowerPoint/Keynote deck
2. PDF One-Pager

### Step 2: Provide Context
You share:
- Audience (who are you presenting to?)
- Goal (what action should they take?)

Gem delivers:

- Design system specifications

- Build instructions

### Step 4: Build & Iterate
You:
- Build in PowerPoint/design software
- Request refinements ("expand slide 5", "make shorter")
- Generate alternative versions
- Apply your brand's visual assets

## ğŸ’¡ Best Practices

### âœ… Do This:

**Be Specific**
```
Bad:  "Create a sales presentation"
Good: "Create a 15-slide sales pitch deck for VP of Sales at mid-market
       SaaS companies about our AI CRM, goal is to book a demo, emphasize
       60% admin time reduction"
```

**Upload Quality References**
- Your best sales deck (not mediocre ones)
- Current brand guidelines
- Templates you want to replicate
- Presentations that got great results

**Iterate Incrementally**
- Start with overall structure
- Then: "Expand slides 3-5 with more detail"
- Then: "Add data visualizations to ROI section"
- Then: "Create alternative opening"

**Use All Three Formats**
- Create full PowerPoint deck first
- Then: "Create a PDF sales slick version of this"
- Then: "Create a one-pager summary"
- Consistent messaging across all formats

### âŒ Don't Do This:

**Don't Be Vague**
- Missing audience, goal, or key message
- "Create a presentation about our company"
- Forces Gem to guess or ask many clarifying questions

**Don't Upload Bad Examples**
- Low-quality reference presentations
- Outdated brand guidelines
- Presentations that didn't work well
- Gem will learn bad patterns

**Don't Expect Instant Perfection**
- First output is a starting point
- Refine and iterate based on your needs
- Provide feedback to improve results

**Don't Forget Your Brand**
- Upload brand guidelines or specify colors/fonts
- Otherwise Gem creates generic (though professional) design
- Brand consistency requires brand input
