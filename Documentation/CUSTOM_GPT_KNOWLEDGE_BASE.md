## 1. BLOOM'S TAXONOMY DETAILED GUIDE

### Action Verbs by Level

**CREATE (9-10/10)** - Highest cognitive level
- Design, develop, build, construct, formulate, propose, plan, compose, devise, generate, invent, produce

**Example Objectives**:
- ✅ "Design AI transformation strategy aligned to business objectives using McKinsey framework"
- ✅ "Develop data governance framework compliant with GDPR/CCPA"
- ✅ "Propose AI Center of Excellence organizational structure"

---

**EVALUATE (8/10)** - Judgment and critique
- Assess, judge, critique, evaluate, compare, recommend, justify, defend, argue, prioritize, rank

**Example Objectives**:
- ✅ "Evaluate data architecture patterns and recommend optimal approach for enterprise scale"
- ✅ "Assess AI systems for bias, fairness, and ethical concerns"
- ✅ "Compare ML approaches and justify selection for business problem"

---

**ANALYZE (7/10)** - Breaking down concepts
- Analyze, compare, contrast, differentiate, organize, deconstruct, examine, investigate, categorize

**Example Objectives**:
- ✅ "Analyze operational processes to identify automation opportunities"
- ✅ "Compare foundation models (GPT-4 vs Claude vs open-source) for use case"
- ✅ "Differentiate between centralized, federated, and data mesh architectures"

---

**APPLY (6/10)** - Using knowledge
- Apply, implement, execute, use, demonstrate, solve, operate, employ, practice

**Example Objectives**:
- ✅ "Implement data governance framework compliant with GDPR"
- ✅ "Apply SPIN selling methodology to AI solution sales"
- ✅ "Execute threat modeling exercise for AI system"

---

**UNDERSTAND (4/10)** - WEAK for executive education
- Explain, describe, summarize, interpret, classify, discuss, paraphrase

**Example (AVOID)**:
- ❌ "Understand AI strategy frameworks"
- ❌ "Explain machine learning concepts"

**How to Fix**: Upgrade to Analyze, Evaluate, or Create
- ✅ "Design AI strategy using Porter's Five Forces framework"

---

**REMEMBER (2/10)** - WEAKEST for executive education
- List, define, recall, identify, name, recognize, memorize

**Example (AVOID)**:
- ❌ "List types of machine learning"
- ❌ "Define data governance"

**How to Fix**: Upgrade to Apply or Analyze
- ✅ "Implement data governance policy for your organization"

## 2. ADULT LEARNING PRINCIPLES (ANDRAGOGY)

### Knowles' 5 Principles - Detailed

#### **Principle 1: Self-Direction**
**Definition**: Adult learners want control over their learning path, pace, and choices.

**Evidence in Curriculum**:
- ✅ Optional modules or electives
- ✅ Self-paced content (async videos, readings)
- ✅ Choice in capstone project topics
- ✅ Multiple pathways to complete program
- ✅ Learner sets own goals

**Scoring**:
- YES (1 point): 3+ self-direction elements present
- NO (0 points): Fixed curriculum, no learner choice

---

#### **Principle 2: Experience Integration**
**Definition**: Adults learn best when new knowledge builds on professional experience.

**Evidence in Curriculum**:
- ✅ Case studies from learner's industry
- ✅ "Apply to your organization" exercises
- ✅ Peer sharing of real-world challenges
- ✅ Capstone using learner's company problem
- ✅ Breakout discussions connecting theory to practice

**Scoring**:
- YES (1 point): 3+ experience integration elements
- NO (0 points): Generic theory, no connection to learner experience

---

#### **Principle 3: Readiness to Learn**
**Definition**: Adults are motivated to learn when content solves current job problems (just-in-time learning).

**Evidence in Curriculum**:
- ✅ Content applies immediately to job
- ✅ Deliverables usable in workplace (e.g., data strategy you can implement)
- ✅ Skills needed for current role (not future hypothetical)
- ✅ Timing aligns with business needs

**Scoring**:
- YES (1 point): Content immediately applicable
- NO (0 points): Theoretical or future-focused only

---

#### **Principle 4: Problem-Centered**
**Definition**: Adults learn through solving real problems, not memorizing theory.

**Evidence in Curriculum**:
- ✅ Capstone project on real business challenge
- ✅ Case studies with decisions to make
- ✅ Simulations and scenario planning
- ✅ Problem-based learning (not lecture-only)
- ✅ Action learning sets (peer problem-solving)

**Scoring**:
- YES (1 point): 3+ problem-centered methods
- NO (0 points): Lecture-only, no application

---

#### **Principle 5: Internal Motivation**
**Definition**: Adults are driven by career advancement, mastery, impact (not grades/compliance).

**Evidence in Curriculum**:
- ✅ Credential valuable for promotion (e.g., "CDAIO Certificate")
- ✅ Portfolio-worthy deliverables
- ✅ Networking with senior peers
- ✅ Skills directly tied to leadership roles
- ✅ Recognition (certificate ceremony, LinkedIn badge)

**Scoring**:
- YES (1 point): Clear career/impact benefits
- NO (0 points): Unclear value proposition

## 3. ASSESSMENT FRAMEWORK DESIGN

### The 4 Assessment Types (All Required)

#### **Type 1: Quizzes (15-20% of grade)**

**Best Practices**:
- 10 questions per session
- 80% passing threshold
- 2 attempts allowed
- Immediate feedback with explanations
- Mix of multiple choice, true/false, matching

**Example Quiz Structure**:
```
Session: AI Strategy Frameworks
Questions: 10
Time limit: 20 minutes
Passing: 8/10 (80%)
Attempts: 2

Sample Questions:
1. Which framework helps prioritize AI use cases by business value and feasibility?
   a) Porter's Five Forces
   b) Value-Feasibility Matrix ✓
   c) SWOT Analysis
   d) BCG Matrix

2. What is the primary goal of AI strategy?
   [Multiple choice with feedback]
```

**Weight Recommendation**: 15-20% of total grade

---

#### **Type 2: Deliverables (25-35% of grade)**

**Best Practices**:
- 3-5 graded deliverables throughout program
- Clear rubrics (4 levels: Below/Meets/Exceeds/Exceptional)
- Business-aligned (usable in workplace)
- 1-2 page format (executive-friendly)
- Due 1 week after session

**Example Deliverables**:
1. **Data Strategy 1-Pager** (Week 5, 10 points)
2. **AI Transformation Roadmap** (Week 10, 25 points)
3. **Customer AI Strategy Proposal** (Week 14, 15 points)
4. **AI CoE Organizational Design** (Week 16, 15 points)

**Weight Recommendation**: 25-35% of total grade

---

#### **Type 3: Capstone (30-40% of grade)**

**Best Practices**:
- Real business problem from learner's organization
- 5-7 page written report + 15-min presentation
- Panel evaluation (faculty + industry experts)
- Peer review component (20% of capstone grade)
- Clear rubric with 5 criteria

**Example Capstone Requirements**:
```
Project: AI Strategic Initiative for Your Organization

Deliverables:
1. Executive summary (2 pages)
2. Technical design document (5-7 pages)
3. ROI model (financial projection)
4. Implementation plan (timeline, resources, risks)
5. 15-minute presentation to panel

Rubric (100 points):
- Business alignment: 25 points
- Technical feasibility: 20 points
- ROI analysis: 20 points
- Implementation plan: 20 points
- Presentation quality: 15 points

Due: Final week of program
```

**Weight Recommendation**: 30-40% of total grade

---

#### **Type 4: Participation (10-15% of grade)**

**Best Practices**:
- Attendance requirement (10+ of 12 sessions)
- Peer discussion contributions (quality, not just quantity)
- Breakout room engagement
- Helping peers (forums, Slack)

**Scoring**:
- Full credit: 10+ sessions, active participation
- Partial credit: 8-9 sessions or passive attendance
- No credit: <8 sessions

**Weight Recommendation**: 10-15% of total grade

---

### Overall Grading Scale

| Grade | Percentage | Criteria |
|-------|-----------|----------|
| **A** | 90-100% | Exceptional: All deliverables exceed expectations, strong capstone |
| **B** | 80-89% | Strong: Meets expectations across all assessments |

| **F** | <70% | Does not meet requirements |

**Certificate Requirements**:
- Minimum 70% overall
- Attendance: 10+ of 12 sessions
- Capstone: Must submit and present

---

### Example Rubric: Data Strategy 1-Pager

| Criterion | Weight | Exceptional (95-100) | Exceeds (85-94) | Meets (70-84) | Below (<70) |
|-----------|--------|---------------------|-----------------|---------------|-------------|
| **Business Alignment** | 30% | Strategy directly tied to 3+ strategic goals with quantified impact | Tied to 2 goals with clear alignment | Mentions business goals generically | No business alignment |
| **Data Architecture** | 25% | Detailed current/future state with specific technologies and migration plan | Current/future state defined with some detail | Basic architecture mentioned | Vague or missing |
| **Governance** | 25% | Comprehensive framework (policies, roles, compliance) with implementation plan | Framework defined with key elements | Basic governance approach | Governance not addressed |
| **Feasibility** | 20% | Realistic timeline, budget, risks identified with mitigation | Timeline and budget provided | Timeline mentioned | No implementation detail |

**Total**: 100 points

---

## 8. GAP ANALYSIS CATEGORIES

### Content Gaps

**Definition**: Missing or outdated curriculum topics

**Common Examples**:
- ❌ No GenAI/LLM content (critical for 2024+)
- ❌ No MLOps/production AI (model monitoring, drift, A/B testing)
- ❌ Missing ethical AI frameworks
- ❌ No AI security/threat modeling
- ❌ Outdated ML content (pre-2020)

**How to Fix**:
- Add session: "Generative AI for Enterprises" (LLMs, RAG, prompt engineering)
- Add session: "MLOps & Production AI" (monitoring, deployment, testing)
- Update case studies to 2023-2024 examples

---

### Pedagogical Gaps

**Definition**: Weak instructional design or learning methods

**Common Examples**:
- ❌ No hands-on labs or technical exercises
- ❌ Lecture-only (no peer collaboration)
- ❌ No case studies or simulations
- ❌ Limited real-world application
- ❌ Weak Bloom's taxonomy (too much Remember/Understand)

**How to Fix**:
- Add 30-min Colab notebook per session (12 labs)
- Add breakout rooms for peer discussion (20 min per session)
- Add 2-3 case studies per module
- Rewrite learning objectives to Evaluate/Create level

---

### Assessment Gaps

**Definition**: Missing or weak evaluation methods

**Common Examples**:
- ❌ No quizzes or knowledge checks
- ❌ Deliverables without rubrics
- ❌ Attendance-only grading
- ❌ No capstone or final project
- ❌ Unclear how learning is measured

**How to Fix**:
- Add 10-question quiz per session (80% passing, 2 attempts)
- Create 4-level rubrics for all deliverables
- Weight assessments: 20% quizzes, 30% deliverables, 35% capstone, 15% participation
- Require 70% overall to earn certificate

---

### Delivery Gaps

**Definition**: Poor format, pacing, or learner experience

**Common Examples**:
- ❌ No pre-work or preparation
- ❌ Sessions too long (>120 min) or too short (<45 min)
- ❌ No async components (everything live)
- ❌ Missing post-program support (alumni community)
- ❌ No mobile-friendly platform

**How to Fix**:
- Add pre-work: 15-min reading + 5-min video per session
- Optimal session length: 90 minutes (60 lecture + 20 activity + 10 Q&A)
- Add async videos for flexible learning
- Create alumni Slack workspace + quarterly meetups
- Ensure mobile-responsive design

---

### Differentiation Gaps

**Definition**: Not unique vs competitors, generic content

**Common Examples**:
- ❌ Same content as MIT/Stanford (no unique IP)
- ❌ Generic curriculum (not industry-tailored)
- ❌ No proprietary frameworks or tools
- ❌ Weak credential/brand value
- ❌ Unclear positioning

**How to Fix**:
- Develop proprietary framework (e.g., "Michigan CAIO Maturity Model")
- Tailor content to specific industries (healthcare AI, financial services AI)
- Partner with unique practitioners (e.g., Fortune 500 CAIOs)
- Position clearly: "Most hands-on CAIO program" or "Only program with XYZ"
