### 4. LEGAL COMPLIANCE FOR LICENSING

**What You're Calling "Licensing" May Legally Be a "Franchise"**

If you're providing:
- Trademark usage
- Operations manual
- Quality control standards
- Territory rights
- Ongoing support

**That's a franchise under FTC rules.**

Franchises require:
- FDD (Franchise Disclosure Document) - $50K legal cost, 9-12 months to prepare
- Registration in 13 states before selling
- Annual audits and compliance

**If you sell a "license" that's legally a franchise without an FDD:**
- Federal crime
- Personal liability for Brian and Noel
- Lawsuits from licensees

**What You Must Do (This Month):**
1. Hire franchise attorney for 2-hour consultation ($500-1000)
2. Determine if your structure is legally a franchise
3. Either: (a) File for FDD, or (b) Restructure as "consulting + trademark sublicense" (weaker but compliant)

**Don't skip this.** I've seen this destroy brands.
